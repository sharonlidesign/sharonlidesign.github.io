Popcorn Web App

Overview

Popcorn, previously Lunchable, is an early-stage startup that helps college students find friends and network by setting them up with people of similar interests. Throughout my time at Popcorn, I was directly involved in shaping its rebrand and launch as a web app that introduces people to others by scheduling virtual group meetups. I worked closely with a fellow UX designer as well as the CEO to create the entire user experience from conception to delivering the final prototype to the engineering team.

Role & Duration

Role: UX Designer

Team: 1 CEO/Project Manager, 5 Engineers, 2 Designers

Timeline: May 2020 – July 2020

Methods: Prototyping (Figma), User Flows (Miro), User Interviews, Usability Testing, Wireframing, Sketching

Final Product: Features I Worked On

Sign Up Process

Providing a standard, intuitive process and several different channels for the user to register through.

Profile Creation

Helping users create engaging, personalized profiles through an enjoyable form filling process.

Scheduling a Meetup

Letting users schedule meetups efficiently while ensuring good matches.

Confirming the Meetup

Introducing users to their group matches in a personable way and actively encouraging them to commit to the meetup.

The Problem

College students want to make new friends online, however they feel anxious about potentially having awkward conversations or getting ignored due to not knowing the other people well enough.

The Solution

Popcorn aims to lift the stress that comes with meeting new people by facilitating high-participation in group meetups, building a sense of commonality early-on, and removing barriers in starting conversations.

Goal

Facilitate high turnout at meetups

Approaches

Organize all meetup logistics for users. Direct users to confirm that they’re participating early on.

Goal

Build a strong sense of group commonality

Approaches

Put together groups based on hobbies and interests. Tell them what they have in common.

Goal

Remove barriers to starting conversation

Approaches

Provide conversation starters. Make chatting and reaching out as convenient as possible.

User Research

To better understand the target user, I interviewed 7 college-aged students. The interviewees ranged from freshmen to seniors at large public universities. I focused on learning about their experiences with finding friends online, especially since a lot of students have to connect with their classmates solely online due to the pandemic.
User Frustrations
Feel awkward using other friend-matching apps because it is difficult to find a “tangible connection” to talk about.
Have low expectations for other friend-matching apps because they do not guarantee a face-to-face meetup or video call.
User Needs
Want to get to know a person more and have a topic in mind before initiating a conversation.
Want to make friends that they can learn from and get advice from in terms of life, school, career, etc.

Concept Development

Mapping Out the Flow
Before I started prototyping, I mapped out the task flow. I focused on ordering the actions in a way that is intuitive, yet it still guides the user enough to alter their behavior, namely increasing their likelihood to sign up and show up to a meetup.
Finding the Right Approach
After deciding the flow, I roughly sketched out what each feature would look like in order to explore how to approach them and organize them into pages that made sense to the user.

Explored how to display an Upcoming Meetup, focusing on what information to display for each meetup.

Drew out a basic form to plan out the ordering and type of questions that would best engage the user.

Planned out how to allow meetup matches to start conversing early on.

Prototyping

Influencing Users to Attend the Meetup
I focused a lot on the “Upcoming Meetup” page because it has the biggest impact on whether or not the user decides to go to a meetup.

Iteration 1

Gives the user a short look at their matches and the option to view more details. Details page emphasizes helping the user learn more about their match’s basic info and interests.

Iteration 2

Also gives the user a short look at their matches with the option to view more details. Details page emphasizes encouraging users to chat with the group.

Iteration 3

Allows the user to see all information on their matches and meetup all in one glance. Introduces matches to the user in a friendly, personal way by providing personalized intros and attendence status right off the bat.

I decided to go with Iteration 3 because it better influences the user to attend the meetup as they will feel like they know the other people better. Overall, this presentation of information is concise yet comprehensive in encouraging the user to learn about and communicate with their matches.
Providing an Enjoyable Video Call Experience
Given that many of the interviewees mentioned how much they dread awkward conversations during first meetings, I wanted to incorporate some kind of conversation starter into the video call. Many users said that they would use this feature a lot during usability testing, further confirming that I should include it.

Iteration 1

User has comprehensive information about others readily available while conversing. The text takes up a lot of space, which was distracting users from the people’s faces.

Iteration 2

Puts the conversation starters in the middle, in the form of cards, gamifying the conversation. Action buttons are rather spread apart on the interface and varied in appearance, requiring users to do a bit more exploration to figure out all the actions.

Iteration 3

All the action buttons are centralized at the bottom, making it easy for the user to find and understand. The conversation starters are at the bottom of the page, as to not obstruct the user’s view of the other people.

I ended up choosing Iteration 3 because compared to the others, it best allows users to put their attention on the people they’re talking to, while still giving them easy, non-obstructive access to conversation assistance if they need it.

Feedback & Usability Testing

Fostering Meetup Participation
I usability-tested by asking users to go through the whole flow, giving them specific tasks along the way. After recieving feedback from users, as well as my team, I made the following changes:

  • Moved the “Confirm Now” button to a more visible and prominent position. This would encourage the user to click this button, thus furthering our number one goal of increasing meetup participation.

  • Changed the green checkmarks to text because users were confused about their meaning during usability testing. Now, users can clearly see which people are confirmed to attend the meetup, which would in turn encourage them to confirm as well.

  • Made the chat notification red in order to make it stand out more, thus encouraging users to click on it and engage with the chat.

Reimagining How Users Connect with Past Meetups
I initially designed this page to show a history of the user’s past meetups. After recieving feedback, I redesigned it with the following changes:

  • Changed “Past Meetups” page into a “Chats” page replacing the meetup cards with chats. This would make it more accessible and effortless for users to engage with past meetup groups, thus furthering our goal of fostering engagement.

  • Removed the chatbox from the “Upcoming Meetup” page and instead put all chats in together in one page in order to create a more centralized place for users to talk to others. During testing, users also confirmed that this format makes them feel more inclined to participate in the group chats.

UI Designs

Redesigning the Profile to be More Engaging
I redesigned the profile page to take up more horizontal space, to let the user to see more info without scrolling. Taking up more horizontal space also allows users to immediately see the images at the bottom, which add the most personalized touch to the profile. Overall, the profile page plays a huge role in how users get to know each other before a meetup, so I really focused on making it expressive, yet standardized.

Takeaways

Throughout this internship, I gained great insight into how to design for increased user engagement. An important design consideration for this app was to influence users to be more proactive and communicative to the people they meet online, so I designed with this goal in mind throughout the entire process. I also had a lot of practice communicating my design justifications to cross-functional team members and received a lot of feedback that helped me make the design more engaging and impactful.
