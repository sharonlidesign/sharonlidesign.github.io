Popcorn Web App

Overview

Popcorn, previously Lunchable, is an early-stage startup that helps college students find friends and network by setting them up with people of similar interests. Throughout my time at Popcorn, I was directly involved in shaping its rebrand and launch as a web app that introduces people to others by scheduling virtual group meetups. I worked closely with a fellow UX designer as well as the CEO to create the entire user experience from conception to delivering the final prototype to the engineering team.

Role & Duration

Role: UX Designer

Team: 1 CEO/Project Manager, 5 Engineers, 2 Designers

Timeline: May 2020 – July 2020

Methods: Prototyping (Figma), User Flows (Miro), User Interviews, Usability Testing, Wireframing, Sketching

Final Product: Features I Worked On

Sign Up Process

Providing a standard, intuitive process and several different channels for the user to register through.

Profile Creation

Helping users create engaging, personalized profiles through an enjoyable form filling process.

Scheduling a Meetup

Letting users schedule meetups efficiently while ensuring good matches.

Confirming the Meetup

Introducing users to their group matches in a personable way and actively encouraging them to commit to the meetup.

Chatting

Giving users the opportunity to engage with current and past meetup groups.

Video Call

Providing a fun meetup room that fosters engaging conversation.

The Problem

College students want to make new friends online, however they feel anxious about potentially having awkward conversations or getting ignored due to not knowing the other people well enough.

The Solution

Popcorn aims to lift the stress that comes with meeting new people by facilitating high-participation in group meetups, building a sense of commonality early-on, and removing barriers in starting conversations.

User Research

To better understand the target user, I interviewed 7 college-aged students with questions that allowed me to delve deeper into their motivations and specific emotions they have when using friend-matching services (e.g. Bumble, OkZoomer, etc.).
User Frustrations
Feel awkward using other friend-matching apps because it is difficult to find a “tangible connection” to talk about.
Have low expectations for other friend-matching apps because they do not guarantee an actual in-person meetup.
User Needs
Want to get to know a person more and have a topic in mind to initiate a conversation.
Want to make friends that they can learn from and get advice from in terms of life, school, career, etc.

Concept Development

Mapping Out the Flow
Before I started prototyping, I mapped out the task flow. I focused on ordering the actions in a way that is intuitive, yet it still guides the user enough to alter their behavior, namely increasing their likelihood to sign up and show up to a meetup.
Finding the Right Approach
After deciding the flow, I roughly sketched out what each feature would look like in order to explore how to approach them and organize them into pages that made sense to the user.

Prototyping

Influencing Users to Commit
I focused a lot on the “Upcoming Meetup” page because it has the biggest impact on whether or not the user decides to go to a meetup. My main considerations were making sure the page introduces the user’s matches in a friendly, personal way while providing all the basic information such as the time and video call link.

Iteration 1

Little information on the initial page. User must click “View Details” to learn more about their matches and communicate with them via chat. Chat at the bottom of the “Details” page.

Iteration 2

Similar to the first, but moved the chat to the top of the “Details” page to encourage the user to start chatting with their matches before the meetup.

Iteration 3

Condenses people’s profiles, chat, and meetup info to one page. “Confirm” button is prominent. Lets the user know which other matches have confirmed to attend the meetup. Condensable chat box.

I decided to go with the third iteration because it lets the user learn more about who they’re meeting right off the bat and encourages users to commit to going to the meetup immediately. It shows the chat in a way that gives the user an option to engage in it. It also lets the user see when someone else has confirmed, which encourages them to confirm as well. Overall, it is concise while still giving the user enough information to make them want to attend the meetup.
Providing an Enjoyable Meetup Experience
Another important page was the video call interface. Given that many college students dread awkward conversations during first meetings, I wanted to incorporate some kind of conversation starter feature into the video call. Many users also expressed interest in this feature during usability testing.

Iteration 1

Info about participants. Chatbox that provides convo starters.

Iteration 2

Gives the user the option to view convo starters and participant info but otherwise hide them.

Iteration 3

Convo starters are at the bottom of the page. Everything is hidden unless sought out.

I ended up with the third iteration because it gives users the option to get a convo starter, while not obstructing their view of the people they’re calling. Afterall, many users said that they would prefer to only see the other participants’s faces but like having the option to see the buttons if they so choose.

Feedback & Usability Testing

Fostering Meetup Participation
After recieving feedback from my team, I decided to move the “Confirm Now” button and time to the center of the page, in order to make it more visible and prominent. This would encourage the user to click this button, thus furthering our number one goal of increasing meetup participation. I also changed the green checkmarks to text because users were confused about their meaning during usability testing. Now, users can clearly see which people are confirmed to attend the meetup, which would in turn encourage them to confirm as well. Finally, I made the chat notification red in order to make it stand out more, thus encouraging users to click on it and engage with the chat.
Reimagining How Users Connect with Past Meetups
I initially designed this page to show a history of the user’s past meetups. After recieving feedback from my manager, I redesigned it into the “Chats” page replacing the meetup cards with chats. This would make it more accessible and effortless for users to engage with past meetup groups, thus furthering our goal of fostering engagement. I also removed the chatbox from the “Upcoming Meetup” page and instead put all chats in together in one page in order to create a more centralized place for users to talk to others. During testing, users also confirmed that this format makes them feel more inclined to participate in the group chats.

UI Designs

Redesigning the Profile to be More Engaging
I redesigned the profile page to take up more horizontal space, to let the user to see more info without scrolling. Taking up more horizontal space also allows users to immediately see the images at the bottom, which add the most personalized touch to the profile. Overall, the profile page plays a huge role in how users get to know each other before a meetup, so I really focused on making it expressive, yet standardized.
Creating a More Delightful Experience
I continued to make other changes to content, colors, and typography in order to create a clearer sense of hierarchy and visual consistency for the user. In particular, I redesigned the UI of the sign up pages to provide a more delightful experience that communicates a sense of fun and sociability.

Takeaways

Throughout this internship, I gained great insight into how to design for increased user engagement. An important design consideration for this app was to influence users to be more proactive and communicative to the people they meet online, so I designed with this goal in mind throughout the entire process. I also had a lot of practice communicating my design justifications to cross-functional team members and received a lot of feedback that helped me make the design more engaging and impactful.
