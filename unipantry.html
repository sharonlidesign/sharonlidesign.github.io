UniPantry Mobile App

Overview

UniPantry is a startup created by Cornell students. It serves to empower students to live healthy lifestyles, beginning with eating nutritious meals. As students ourselves, we understand the struggle to eat nutritiously while staying within money and time constraints. I am currently working with the product managers to design and launch the first iteration of the mobile app.

Role & Duration

Role: Product Designer

Team: 2 Product Managers, 7 Engineers, 2 Designers

Timeline: February 2020 – August 2020

Methods: Prototyping (Figma), Usability Testing, Wireframing

Final Product: Features I Worked On

Recipe Filter

Assisting users in personalizing their recipe search.

Grocery List

Creating a way for users to add and delete items from their grocery list with ease.

Grocery History

Allowing users to view their past purchases and add them back to their grocery list.

Pantry

Helping users to keep track of the food in their pantry.

The Problem

Millions of college students who live off-campus want to eat healthy, fulfilling meals, but cannot because of time and budget constraints.

The Solution

The UniPantry app helps students plan and cook their own meals based on their budget and nutrition requirements, while learning money and time saving skills along the way.

Prototyping Iterations

After designing an initial prototype, I made changes to the app based on feedback from my fellow designer, product manager, and users.
Improving the Visual Hierarchy of the Grocery List
In order to make the Grocery List more visually consistent, I decided to remove the pictures of food. Although they make the page more exciting to look at, I discovered that they do not make a difference in the user’s understanding and add a layer of difficulty for our software team. I also decided to extend the grocery categories to cover the width of the screen, in order to make the visual separation of the category sections more clear. The labeling of the different categories are important to users because it helps them efficiently shop at different sections of the grocery store, one at a time.
Making Grocery List Intuitive to Edit
To make the deleting affordance more intuitive, I changed the checking action to a left-swiping action instead. This also helps the user not get confused about which side of the grocery card to click when checking off an item.
Improving the Accessibility of Recipe Filtering
I redesigned the filter function to be more suited to the mobile screen, enlarging the selection area while conserving space and clearly indicating the state of the filter.
